Jamgon Kongtrul Dam Ngag Dzo

གདམས་ངག་མཛོད

Volumes: 18
Versions: W20877

Jamgon Kongtrul Lodro Thaye (1813-1899) the third pillar of the Rime tradition was the author of five collections or compilations of teachings which exemplified the entire Rime movement. The most important collection brings together the methods for practice of the eight Great Chariots into which all of the Buddhist traditions of Tibet can be structured: the Nyingma; the Kadam; the Lamdre; the Marpa Kagyu; the Shangpa Kagyu; the Shiche and Cho; the Kalachakra; and the Ugyen Nyendrub.
